> Yes, there are several competitors to Venmo and Cash App that offer similar peer-to-peer (P2P) payment services. Here are some notable alternatives based on current information:
> 
> 1. **Zelle**: A popular choice for instant, fee-free transfers within the U.S., Zelle integrates directly with many major bank apps, allowing seamless transfers between bank accounts using just an email or phone number. It’s ideal for users prioritizing speed and no fees, but it’s limited to U.S. bank accounts and doesn’t support credit card payments.[](https://www.jotform.com/blog/venmo-alternatives/)[](https://wise.com/us/blog/venmo-alternative)[](https://paymentcloudinc.com/blog/venmo-alternatives/)
> 
> 2. **PayPal**: As Venmo’s parent company, PayPal offers robust features, including international transfers to over 120 countries and buyer protection for online purchases. It charges a 2.9% + $0.30 fee for credit/debit card transactions but is free for bank account transfers. It’s a versatile option for both personal and business use.[](https://www.jotform.com/blog/venmo-alternatives/)[](https://rates.fm/payment-systems/best-venmo-alternative/)[](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2021/07/21/best-p2p-payment-apps/)
> 
> 3. **Apple Cash**: A secure option for Apple users, Apple Cash allows P2P payments via iMessage and integrates with Apple devices. It’s free for debit card/bank account transfers and uses Face ID or Touch ID for security. However, it’s limited to Apple ecosystem users.[](https://www.chime.com/blog/venmo-alternatives/)[](https://wise.com/us/blog/venmo-alternative)
> 
> 4. **Google Pay**: Available for both Android and iPhone users, Google Pay supports P2P payments, contactless payments, and loyalty card storage. It’s free for debit card/bank account transfers, with a 1.5% fee for debit card payments. It’s a strong choice for those in the Google ecosystem but has limited international transfer options.[](https://www.chime.com/blog/venmo-alternatives/)[](https://wise.com/us/blog/apps-like-cash-app)[](https://finmasters.com/venmo-alternatives/)
> 
> 5. **Wise**: Ideal for international transfers, Wise supports payments to over 140 countries with mid-market exchange rates and low, transparent fees. Unlike Venmo and Cash App, it’s not focused on social payments but excels for cross-border transactions.[](https://wise.com/us/blog/venmo-alternative)[](https://wise.com/us/blog/apps-like-cash-app)
> 
> 6. **Revolut**: Offers P2P payments, multi-currency accounts, and crypto trading. It’s a good alternative for users needing international capabilities and additional financial tools, though it may have fees for certain transactions.[](https://wise.com/us/blog/apps-like-cash-app)[](https://www.narolainfotech.com/blogs/apps-like-cash-app/)
> 
> 7. **BOSS Revolution**: A strong option for international transfers, it offers competitive rates and multiple delivery methods (bank deposit, mobile wallet, cash pickup). It’s less focused on social payments but great for global remittances.[](https://www.bossrevolution.com/en-us/blog/cash-app-alternatives)[](https://www.sevensquaretech.com/best-apps-like-venmo-and-alternatives/)
> 
> 8. **Gerald**: A lesser-known but emerging competitor, Gerald offers fee-free P2P transfers, instant cash advances, and Buy Now, Pay Later options. It’s designed for users seeking comprehensive financial tools without Venmo’s instant transfer fees.[](https://joingerald.com/blog/venmo-competitors)
> 
> Each of these apps has unique strengths: Zelle for fee-free domestic transfers, PayPal for global reach, Apple Cash and Google Pay for device integration, Wise and Revolut for international payments, and Gerald for fee-free financial tools. Your choice depends on your needs—domestic vs. international, social features, or additional financial services. Always verify security features like encryption and two-factor authentication before using any app.[](https://www.sevensquaretech.com/best-apps-like-venmo-and-alternatives/)[](https://joingerald.com/blog/venmo-competitors)

> ### Do Cash App and Venmo Have a Monopoly on Free Peer-to-Peer Money Exchange?
> 
> No, Cash App and Venmo do not have a monopoly on free peer-to-peer (P2P) money exchange. While they are among the most popular P2P payment apps in the U.S., several other competitors offer similar free P2P transfer services, preventing a monopoly. A monopoly would imply that Cash App and Venmo control the market to the extent that they can dictate terms, prices, or exclude competition, which isn’t the case given the presence of viable alternatives. Below is an analysis:
> 
> - **Market Presence**: Cash App and Venmo are major players, with Venmo boasting nearly 90 million accounts and Cash App around 44 million monthly active users as of recent data. However, other apps like Zelle (used by over 51% of U.S. consumers in some surveys), PayPal (57% of U.S. adults), and Apple Cash have significant market share, indicating a competitive landscape.[](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2022/09/08/payment-apps-like-venmo-and-cash-app-bring-convenience-and-security-concerns-to-some-users/)[](https://tech.yahoo.com/apps/articles/comparing-paypal-venmo-zelle-cash-090000376.html)
> - **Free P2P Transfers**: Both Cash App and Venmo offer free P2P transfers when using a linked bank account, debit card, or app balance. However, competitors like Zelle, Apple Cash, and Google Pay also provide free transfers for similar methods:
>   - **Zelle**: Offers instant, fee-free transfers between U.S. bank accounts, often integrated directly with major bank apps. It’s widely used for its speed and no-fee structure.[](https://wise.com/us/blog/venmo-alternative)
>   - **Apple Cash**: Free for P2P transfers via debit card or bank account within the Apple ecosystem, with instant transfers.[](https://tech.yahoo.com/apps/articles/comparing-paypal-venmo-zelle-cash-090000376.html)
>   - **Google Pay**: Free for bank account or debit card transfers, though it lacks a dedicated debit card.[](https://wise.com/us/blog/venmo-alternative)
>   - **PayPal**: Free for domestic P2P transfers using a bank account or PayPal balance, though credit/debit card transfers incur a 2.9% + $0.30 fee.[](https://thecollegeinvestor.com/41686/cashapp-venmo-zelle-paypal/)
>   - **Gerald**: Offers fee-free P2P transfers, similar to Cash App and Venmo, with a debit card option. 
> - **Barriers to Monopoly**: The P2P payment market is highly competitive due to low switching costs (users can easily download and try multiple apps), widespread availability of free transfer options, and diverse features (e.g., international transfers, crypto trading, or social features). Regulatory oversight, such as Consumer Financial Protection Bureau warnings about fraud and lack of FDIC insurance, also keeps the market in check.[](https://www.moneydigest.com/1443736/pros-cons-money-transfer-cash-app-vs-venmo/)[](https://www.consumerreports.org/money/digital-payments/peer-to-peer-payment-apps-comparison-a5999129619/)
> - **Critical Perspective**: While Cash App and Venmo are dominant in the U.S. for social and casual P2P payments, their lack of international reach (except Cash App’s limited U.S.-U.K. transfers) and competition from bank-backed Zelle and global players like PayPal and Wise prevent monopolistic control. Claims of market dominance may be overstated by mainstream narratives, as user adoption of alternatives (e.g., 59% of Black Americans use Cash App vs. 38% for Venmo) shows diverse preferences.[](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2022/09/08/payment-apps-like-venmo-and-cash-app-bring-convenience-and-security-concerns-to-some-users/)
> 
> ### Who Owns Cash App and Venmo?
> 
> - **Cash App**: Owned by **Block, Inc.** (formerly Square, Inc.), a financial technology company founded by Jack Dorsey. Block focuses on payment solutions, including Cash App’s ecosystem of P2P transfers, debit cards, and investment options.[](https://surveysparrow.com/blog/venmo-vs-cash-app/)[](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/01/venmo-gaining-ground-in-payments-as-cash-app-struggles.html)
> - **Venmo**: Owned by **PayPal Holdings, Inc.**, a global leader in digital payments. PayPal acquired Venmo in 2013 through its purchase of Braintree and has since integrated it into its broader payment network, emphasizing social payment features.[](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/01/venmo-gaining-ground-in-payments-as-cash-app-struggles.html)[](https://thecollegeinvestor.com/41686/cashapp-venmo-zelle-paypal/)
> 
> ### Key Takeaways
> - **No Monopoly**: Cash App and Venmo face robust competition from Zelle, PayPal, Apple Cash, Google Pay, and others, all offering free P2P transfers under similar conditions. The market is fragmented, with user choice driven by features, device compatibility, and trust.
> - **Ownership**: Cash App is owned by Block, Inc., and Venmo by PayPal Holdings, Inc., two separate entities with no shared ownership, further undermining any monopoly claims.
> - **Choosing an App**: If free P2P transfers and a debit card are priorities, Cash App, Venmo, PayPal, Apple Cash, Revolut, and Gerald are closest in functionality. For purely free transfers without a card, Zelle is a strong contender.
