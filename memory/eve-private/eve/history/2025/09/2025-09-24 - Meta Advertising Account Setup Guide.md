# Meta Advertising Account Setup Guide

> ### Setting Up a Meta Business Manager Account for Advertising
> 
> Meta Business Manager (now part of Meta Business Suite) is the central hub for managing advertising on Facebook and Instagram. It allows you to create and organize ad accounts, track performance, and securely grant access to team members or partners without sharing personal logins. You'll need a personal Facebook account to get started, as it's used for verification. Here's a step-by-step guide to set it up:
> 
> 1. **Log In to Meta Business Suite**: Go to [business.facebook.com](https://business.facebook.com) and log in with your personal Facebook account. If you don't have one, create a personal Facebook profile first (this is required for identity verification).
> 
> 2. **Create a Business Portfolio**: 
>    - Hover over your profile picture in the left-hand menu (under the Meta logo) and select "Create a new Business Portfolio" or "All Tools" > "Business Settings."
>    - Enter your business name (use your company's public name, avoiding special characters), your full name as the admin, and a business email address. This email will receive billing updates and alerts.
> 
> 3. **Verify Your Business**: Provide details like your business address, website, and tax ID if prompted. Meta may require additional verification (e.g., documents) for higher spending limits later.
> 
> 4. **Create an Ad Account**:
>    - In Business Settings, go to "Ad Accounts" under Accounts.
>    - Click "Add" > "Create a New Ad Account."
>    - Set up billing: Add a payment method (credit card or PayPal) and timezone/currency. This ties spending to your business, not your personal profile.
>    - Note: Ad accounts have spending limits based on history; start small and scale up.
> 
> 5. **Set Up Essential Tools for Advertising**:
>    - **Meta Pixel**: In Business Settings > Data Sources > Pixels > Add. Install the Pixel code on your website to track conversions (e.g., purchases).
>    - **Connect Assets**: Link your Facebook Page and Instagram professional account under Pages/Instagram Accounts.
>    - **Domain Verification**: Verify your website domain in Business Settings > Brand Safety > Domains to enable advanced tracking like Conversions API (CAPI).
> 
> Once set up, you can access Ads Manager from the dashboard to create campaigns. For client work, create separate ad accounts per client within your Business Manager to keep things organized.
> 
> ### Allowing Personal Emails Access to Client Accounts
> 
> To manage multiple clients securely, treat your agency/team as a "partner" in Meta's system. This lets you request access to a client's ad account (and other assets) via their Business Manager ID, without them sharing passwords. Your team members (using their personal emails tied to Facebook profiles) can then be granted access within your Business Manager. Avoid using work emails alone, as Meta requires a personal Facebook profile for login and policy compliance. Here's how:
> 
> #### Option 1: Request Access to a Client's Ad Account (As a Partner)
> 1. **Get the Client's Business ID**: Ask your client to log into their Business Manager > Business Settings > scroll to the bottom for their Business ID (a 10-15 digit number). If they don't have one, guide them to create it (see setup above).
> 
> 2. **Add the Client as a Partner**:
>    - In your Business Settings, go to "Partners" under Users.
>    - Click "Add" > "Request access to assets from a partner" (or "Ask a partner to share their assets").
>    - Enter the client's name, email, and Business ID.
>    - Select the assets (e.g., specific ad account) and permission level: 
>      - **Advertiser**: View and manage ads/campaigns.
>      - **Analyst**: View performance data.
>      - **Admin**: Full control (use cautiously).
>    - Click "Get Started" and send the request. The client reviews and approves via email.
> 
> 3. **Handle Approval**: The client goes to their Business Settings > Partners > confirms your request and assigns access. Once approved, the client's ad account appears in your Ads Manager.
> 
> #### Option 2: Add Team Members (Personal Emails) to Your Business Manager for Client Access
> Once you have client access, grant it to your team:
> 1. In Business Settings > "People" under Users.
> 2. Click "Add People" or "Invite People."
> 3. Enter the team member's personal email (tied to their Facebook profile).
> 4. Assign roles and assets:
>    - Select the client's ad account from the list.
>    - Choose access level (e.g., Partial Access for ad management).
>    - Decide if access is permanent or temporary.
> 5. Click "Send Invite." They'll receive an email to accept (logging in with their personal Facebook).
> 
> #### Best Practices for Agencies/Teams
> - **Organization**: Create a separate ad account for each client in your Business Manager to avoid mix-ups.
> - **Billing**: Always tie billing to the client's payment method in their ad account—don't use yours to avoid liability.
> - **Security**: Only grant minimal permissions needed. Use two-factor authentication on all personal Facebook accounts.
> - **If Client Lacks Setup**: Use tools like Leadsie to automate requests, which can prompt them to create a Business Manager during the process.
> - **Limits**: If you hit ad account limits, contact Meta support via Business Settings > Help.
> 
> This setup keeps personal and business activities separate while enabling collaboration. If you run into errors (e.g., unconfirmed payments), ensure all assets are admin-verified. For official help, visit Meta's Business Help Center.
