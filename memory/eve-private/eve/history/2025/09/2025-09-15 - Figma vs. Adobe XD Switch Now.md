> ### Why Figma Over XD for Your Quick Project?
> - **Speed and AI Boost**: Figma's AI features (like auto-generating prototypes from prompts, renaming layers, or suggesting design variations) are a game-changer for rapid app prototyping. They're built right into the Design toolbar and can shave hours off ideation and iteration—perfect if you're racing to finish. Users report it's great for quick mockups without replacing your creative input. XD doesn't have anything comparable, and its interactions feel clunky by comparison in recent benchmarks.
>   
> - **Ease of Learning (It's Not 10x Harder)**: Coming from XD, the transition is straightforward—both use vector-based interfaces, auto-layout, and prototyping flows. Figma's interface is more modern and flexible, but the learning curve is low: You could get proficient in a day or two with free tutorials (e.g., Figma's own beginner guides or YouTube's 2025 AI-focused ones). Many XD users say it's "worth the switch" despite initial familiarity with XD, as Figma's real-time previews and components make app flows faster once you're in.
> 
> - **Long-Term Value**: Figma is actively updated with features like better dev handoff and integrations (e.g., with Jira or Slack), which XD lacks. For app design, Figma excels in responsive prototypes and micro-interactions, often outperforming XD in collaborative scenarios—even if you're solo now, it future-proofs your workflow.
> 
> The only real downside? If XD feels 10x easier purely due to muscle memory, the first hour in Figma might frustrate you. But that's temporary—most comparisons in 2025 call Figma superior overall for flexibility and output quality.
> 
> ### Pricing: You Might Not Even Need to Pay
> Figma's Starter plan is free forever and covers unlimited drafts, UI kits/templates for app design, and basic file inspection—plenty for a quick solo project. It includes limited AI credits (enough for prototyping bursts). If you hit limits or want unlimited AI (3,000+ credits/month) and team libraries, upgrade to Professional at $12/user/month (billed annually) or $16 monthly. No commitment needed; start free and scale if it clicks.
>
