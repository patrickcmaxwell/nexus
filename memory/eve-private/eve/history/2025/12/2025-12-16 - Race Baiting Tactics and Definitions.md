# Race Baiting: Tactics and Definitions

> [!INFO] **User** (2025-12-16 19:48:30)
> What is race bating?

> [!ABSTRACT] **Grok** (2025-12-16 19:48:46)
> **Race baiting** (sometimes spelled "race-baiting") is a term that refers to the deliberate act of inciting racial tensions, prejudice, or anger toward a particular racial group, often for political, social, or personal gain. It typically involves exploiting racial stereotypes, fears, or divisions to provoke strong emotional reactions and rally support.
> 
> ### Common Definitions
> - **Encouraging racism or racial anger** — Intentionally stirring up hatred or resentment against members of a racial group, usually through provocative statements, ads, or rhetoric (e.g., from dictionaries like Merriam-Webster and Cambridge).
> - **Exploiting racial issues for advantage** — Using race to influence voters, audiences, or public opinion, such as through "dog-whistle" politics (subtle cues that appeal to prejudices) or overt attacks.
> - **Historical context** — The term originated in the early 20th century (around the 1920s) to describe efforts by politicians or media to stoke hatred against racial minorities, often white supremacist rhetoric.
> 
> ### Usage and Controversy
> The term is highly politicized and can be used in different ways:
> - Traditionally, it describes actions that promote racism against marginalized groups (e.g., fear-mongering ads implying criminality in certain races).
> - In modern discourse (especially in conservative circles), it is sometimes applied to accusations of racism or discussions of racial injustice, claiming they unnecessarily inflame tensions or "play the race card."
> 
> In either case, it is generally seen as a divisive tactic that prioritizes exploitation over constructive dialogue.
