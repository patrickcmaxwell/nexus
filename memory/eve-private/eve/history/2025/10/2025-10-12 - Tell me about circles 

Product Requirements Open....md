> ### What Is an LLC?
> A Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a flexible business structure that combines the liability protection of a corporation (shielding your personal assets from business debts and lawsuits) with the tax simplicity of a partnership or sole proprietorship. It's popular for small businesses, freelancers, and startups because it's easy to form, manage, and dissolve. LLCs can be single-member (just you) or multi-member, and they're taxed by default as pass-through entities—meaning profits and losses flow to your personal tax return, avoiding double taxation.
> 
> **Important Note:** LLC formation rules vary by state, so the exact process, fees, and timelines depend on where you're forming it (e.g., $50 in Missouri vs. $500 in Massachusetts). Always check your state's Secretary of State website for specifics. This guide is a general overview based on 2025 standards; consult a lawyer or accountant for personalized advice. As of March 2025, the federal Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) reporting requirement has been eliminated, simplifying things—no need to file BOI reports with FinCEN.
> 
> ### Step-by-Step Guide to Starting an LLC
> Here's a consolidated 7-step process drawn from reliable sources. You can DIY for free (plus state fees, typically $100–$300) or use a service like LegalZoom or Northwest Registered Agent for $0–$300 plus fees to handle paperwork.
> 
> | Step | Description | Key Tips & Costs |
> |------|-------------|------------------|
> | **1. Choose Your State** | Decide where to form your LLC—usually your home state unless there's a tax or legal advantage elsewhere (e.g., Delaware for privacy). | No cost to decide. If operating in multiple states, you'll need to "foreign qualify" later ($100–$300 per state). |
> | **2. Pick a Unique Name** | Brainstorm a name that includes "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company." Check availability via your state's business search tool (e.g., Secretary of State site). Reserve it if needed. | Free to check; reservation fees: $10–$50. Avoid restricted words like "bank" without approval. You can file a DBA ("Doing Business As") later for trade names ($10–$100). |
> | **3. Appoint a Registered Agent** | Designate someone (you, a friend, or a service) to receive legal documents. They must have a physical address in the state (no P.O. boxes) and be available during business hours. | Free if DIY; services like Northwest: $125/year. > | **4. File Articles of Organization** | Submit formation documents (called Articles of Organization or Certificate of Formation) to your state's Secretary of State.
