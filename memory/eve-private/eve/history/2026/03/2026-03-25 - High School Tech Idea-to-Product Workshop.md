> **High School Tech Business Workshop Agenda**  
> **Theme: From Wild Idea to Real Product (with AI as your co-founder)**  
> **Total time: 90–120 minutes** (flexible — can run short or add 30 min for beta bug hunt)
> 
> ### Agenda (with exact flow + timing)
> 
> | Time | Segment | What Happens | Who Leads |
> |------|---------|--------------|-----------|
> | 0–5 min | Welcome + Mindset Reset | Quick intro + the ONE thing they’ll remember | Patrick & Londynn |
> | 5–10 min | The Full Cycle in 60 Seconds | Show the exact loop they’re about to live: Concept → Requirements → Critique → Refine → Repeat | Patrick |
> | 10–15 min | Live Demo (optional but powerful) | Patrick & Londynn disagree on a real example, use AI live to resolve it | Both |
> | 15–25 min | Round 1: Raw Pitches | Groups of 3 pitch (2 min max, investor-style — no slides needed) | Students |
> | 25–50 min | The Grill + Live Requirements | Patrick & Londynn ask the 8 questions; Vera (AI) pokes holes and generates requirements in real time | Patrick, Londynn, Vera |
> | 50–60 min | Refinement Time | Groups rewrite one-sentence idea + MVP scope using AI | Students (AI open) |
> | 60–70 min | Round 2: Refined Pitches | Same groups, 90-second pitch of the tightened version | Students |
> | 70–80 min | Key Teaching Moment + Debrief | Patrick & Londynn show real disagreement → resolution; hammer “Ideas are cheap, execution is everything” | Both |
> | 80–90 min | Optional Beta App Bug Hunt + Next Steps | Show live beta → let them break it and report; talk secure non-profit environment | All |
> | 90–120 min | Open Q&A + “Build With Us” | Anyone who wants to keep going gets a direct path | All |
> 
> ### Talking Points (what to actually say — copy/paste friendly)
> 
> #### 1. Welcome + Mindset Reset (0–5 min)
> - “You’re not here to learn how to code. You’re here to learn how the real tech world turns a random idea into something people actually pay for.”
> - “Most adults will tell you ‘ideas are worthless.’ We’re going to prove it live — and then show you how to make the ones that aren’t worthless.”
> - “Today you get to watch two adults disagree in public, argue like normal humans, and use AI to get unstuck in real time. That’s the real skill.”
> 
> #### 2. The Full Cycle (5–10 min)
> - Draw or project the loop:  
>   **Raw Concept → Requirements → Brutal Critique → Refine → Repeat**
> - “We’re going to run this cycle twice in one hour. Real startups do it 50 times before launch.”
> - “AI isn’t here to build for you. AI is your ruthless co-founder that never gets tired and catches your blind spots.”
> 
> #### 3. Live Demo of Disagreement (10–15 min — HIGHLY RECOMMENDED)
> - Pick a simple idea (example: “AI that writes your homework”).
> - Patrick: “This is genius!”  
>   Londynn: “This is terrible — schools will ban it instantly.”
> - Then both turn to AI (Vera) live and ask: “What’s the first thing that breaks?” Watch the requirements appear.
> - Narrate: “See? We just saved ourselves three months of building the wrong thing.”
> 
> #### 4. The 8 Questions (use these verbatim — they are gold)
> Print or project these so every group knows what’s coming:
> 
> 1. Who actually needs this? (Not “everyone” — name the exact person.)
> 2. Why would they pay for it instead of something that already exists?
> 3. What’s the first thing that breaks when you try to build it?
> 4. How do you know people want this before you spend months building it?
> 5. What’s the one thing you’d need to prove first?
> 6. If you had to launch in two weeks with half the features, what stays?
> 7. Who else is building this and what are they missing?
> 8. What would make you quit on this idea?
> 
> #### 5. Key Teaching Moment (70–80 min)
> - “You just watched us disagree, get frustrated, and still land on something better. That’s the entire job.”
> - “Ideas are cheap. Execution is everything. AI makes thinking 10x faster so you can spend your time building instead of guessing.”
> - “As kids you feel more free than rules can control… but you also need direction. That’s why we’re giving you both today: total freedom to dream + a secure, moderated path if you actually want to build.”
> 
> #### 6. Secure Environment / Non-Profit Wrapper (mention in last 5 min)
> - “If any group wants to keep going after today, we have a safe zone set up: a non-profit structure where one parent/adult is the designated moderator.”
> - “Everything you share stays inside that protected group. No random strangers, no public internet posts, no risk. You get freedom + guardrails.”
> 
> ### Quick Facilitation Tips for You & Londynn
> - Keep pitches brutally short (timer on).
> - Vera stays on screen the whole time so students see AI being used live.
> - When a group says “everyone,” immediately hit them with question 1.
> - Celebrate the group that takes the hardest critique the best — that’s the real winner.
> - End with: “If you leave here with one tightened idea and the confidence to actually start building, we did our job.”
