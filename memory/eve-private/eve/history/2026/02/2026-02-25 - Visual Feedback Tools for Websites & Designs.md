> [!ABSTRACT] **Grok** (2026-02-25 15:23:15)
> **Pastel (usepastel.com) is a solid, lightweight visual feedback tool** aimed at web designers, developers, agencies, and freelancers. It lets you share a link to a "canvas" where clients (and your team) can leave pinned comments, annotations, and approvals directly on live websites, PDFs, images, social mockups, videos, wireframes, or site maps. No client logins or heavy setup required in most cases—just share the link, and they comment in real time. Features include tagging/organizing feedback, real-time collab, copy editing on the fly, responsiveness testing, deadlines, private team comments, and unlimited guests on all plans.
> 
> It shines for quick, frictionless rounds on live sites or static assets, cutting back-and-forth emails/Slack/screenshots. Testimonials and reviews (G2 ~4.6/5, Capterra positive) repeatedly praise its simplicity, speed, and time savings (e.g., "cut approval time in half," "saved hours per week"). It's browser-based, works on any device, and feels very client-friendly.
> 
> **Pricing** (as of recent 2025–2026 sources):  
> - **Basic/Free**: 1 user, unlimited comments/share links, but with a 72-hour commenting window (comments auto-close after 3 days—creates urgency but can frustrate slow clients; paid plans remove this).  
> - **Pro**: ~$35/mo for 2 users.  
> - **Team**: ~$119/mo starting at 5 users.  
> - **Enterprise**: ~$450/mo for 10+ users (custom features/support).  
> Annual billing likely saves money; unlimited guests across plans.
> 
> ### Pitfalls of Pastel
> It's excellent for small/simple workflows but has clear limitations that agencies or growing teams often hit (mentioned across reviews, comparison blogs like LaunchTheDamnThing, HuddleKit, Medium roundups, and DesignRush):
> 
> - **Scalability and per-user limits**: Cheap tiers cap team members tightly (only 2 on Pro). Adding people gets expensive fast compared to flat-rate or unlimited-guest competitors.
> - **72-hour window on free plan**: Forces quick feedback but can close threads prematurely; workaround exists but isn't seamless.
> - **Limited task/project management**: Comments stay as comments. No native Kanban board, auto-task creation, status tracking, or assignee workflows. You still chase implementation manually.
> - **Fewer/deep integrations**: Basic (maybe Slack/email notifications), but no strong 2-way sync with Jira, Asana, ClickUp, Linear, etc., like heavier tools. No session replay or advanced bug metadata.
> - **Not full-featured for complex projects**: Lacks video/screencast feedback native to some rivals, granular version history, or heavy automation. Some users outgrow it once projects involve multiple sites, ongoing maintenance, or technical QA.
> - **Smaller ecosystem/user base**: Newer/lighter tool, so fewer community resources or third-party add-ons vs. established players.
> - **Other minor**: Relies on link-sharing (great for ease, but less "always-on" than a persistent widget on staging sites). Some comparisons note it doesn't capture hidden elements as well in screenshots.
> 
> Overall, it's not buggy or overly complex—reviews love the interface—but it's "lightweight by design," so it won't replace a full collaboration/project system. If your clients are fast and projects are straightforward website revisions, it's fine. For design-heavy or multi-asset work with slower clients or bigger teams, many switch.
> 
> ### Better or Broader Options (Especially Beyond Just Websites)
> You specifically want tools that handle **designs** (mockups, prototypes, PDFs, images) **and** websites (live/staging), plus general change-request workflows. Here are stronger alternatives categorized by fit. All support visual pinning/annotations and client ease. I prioritized ones that cover **both** websites + designs, task management, and integrations (2025–2026 data from official sites, G2/Capterra, comparison articles).
> 
> #### 1. All-in-One / Hybrid Tools (Best for Websites + Designs + Task Management)
> These replace Pastel entirely and handle live sites **plus** design files in one place—ideal for your "not just website" need.
> 
> - **Webvizio** (strong Pastel alternative, often called "all-in-one cheaper/better"):  
>   Visual feedback on live websites/apps, images, PDFs, design files (JPG/PNG/AI/EPS/SVG). Includes task assignment, Kanban boards, due dates, priorities, screencast video feedback, roles (viewer/assignee for clients), notifications (Slack/email), and Zapier/Jira/ClickUp/Trello integrations. No 72h limit. Super affordable and feature-packed for agencies.  
>   **Pricing**: Solo ~$8–10/mo; Starter ~$28–35/mo (5 users, 10 projects); Advanced ~$76–95/mo (20 users, 100 projects); extra users cheap (~$4–7/mo). Annual discounts.  
>   **Why better**: More power than Pastel at lower cost; covers full design-to-live cycle; built-in task mgmt. Great if you want one tool.
> 
> - **BugHerd** (top-recommended for agencies, more robust than Pastel):  
>   Point-click-pin on live websites (staging/live), auto-captures screenshots + full tech metadata (browser, device, OS, resolution). Supports PDFs, images, Figma files, and video feedback. Turns every comment into trackable tasks on a built-in Kanban board. Unlimited guests/clients (no logins), deadlines, permissions, public widget for post-launch. Deep integrations (Jira, Asana, ClickUp, Trello, Slack, GitHub, Zapier, etc.—2-way sync). AI-assisted features in beta.  
>   **Pricing**: Starts ~$39–50/mo Standard (5 team members, unlimited projects/guests); Studio ~$59–80 (10 members); Premium ~$109–150 (25 members). Annual = 2 months free. 7–14 day trial, 60-day money-back.  
>   **Why better**: Handles websites + designs seamlessly; actual project management (not just comments); reduces rework dramatically (reviews cite 93% faster delivery). Perfect upgrade if you want feedback → tasks → done without extra tools.
> 
> - **Marker.io**:  
>   Similar visual annotation + bug reporting on websites (code embed or extension). Heavy on tech details, session replay (watch user sessions), attachments, comments. Strong 2-way integrations (Jira, Asana, etc.). Good for QA/maintenance alongside designs.  
>   **Pricing**: Starter ~$39/mo (5 members, unlimited sites/feedback); higher Team/Business plans. Agency plan ~$129/mo for 15 members. 15-day trial.  
>   **Why better**: Excellent for technical changes/bugs; scales well for ongoing client sites.
> 
> #### 2. Design-Focused Tools (Excellent for Mockups/Prototypes + Some Web Support)
> Use these for the design phase, then pair with one above for live sites.
> 
> - **Figma** (best pure design feedback tool):  
>   Real-time collab, built-in commenting/pinning on prototypes/mockups, version history, branching, interactive prototypes. Invite clients as "commenters" or "viewers" (no edit access). Works for UI/UX, wireframes, etc. Can embed/share for basic website previews.  
>   **Pricing**: Free for starters (unlimited personal files, limited team); Professional ~$12–20/editor/mo for advanced collab/teams.  
>   **Why better for designs**: Native to most modern design workflows; faster iteration than separate tools. Limitation: Not ideal for annotating fully live/deployed client sites (use screenshots or pair with BugHerd/Webvizio).
> 
> - **Filestage** (centralized proofing/approval for any creative work):  
>   Upload/share any files (designs, PDFs, videos, website screenshots/mockups, images). Version comparison, annotations, approval workflows, due dates, reviewer groups, team-only comments, custom branding. Unlimited reviewers/clients. AI reviewers/automations on higher plans.  
>   **Pricing**: Free limited (1 project); Starter/Basic ~$109–199/mo; Business ~$329/mo (unlimited projects, advanced features). 30-day trial. Flat-rate (not per user).  
>   **Why better**: Handles **everything** non-website (designs heavy) + web assets; structured approvals reduce "vague change" requests.
> 
> #### 3. Other Strong Mentions / Complementary Approaches
> - **Ruttl**: Very similar to Pastel (fast visual on websites + more), cheaper entry (~$10/mo starter in some reports), device previews. Some reliability complaints in older tests—check current trials.
> - **Lightweight/free hybrids**: Loom (video walkthroughs of sites/designs + timestamped comments) + shared Google Drive/Notion/Miro for async feedback. Or native platform tools (Webflow comments if using that builder).
> - **Full project suites**: If you want everything in one (feedback + tasks + docs), look at ClickUp/Monday.com with visual comment plugins, but they're less seamless for on-page pinning than dedicated tools.
> 
> ### My Overall Recommendations
> - **Start here if you like Pastel's simplicity but want more**: Try **Webvizio** first—it's the closest "better Pastel" with design support, tasks, and low cost. Or **BugHerd** if your team uses Jira/Asana and needs feedback auto-converted to tasks.
> - **Design-heavy workflow**: Figma for creation/iteration + BugHerd or Webvizio for live website changes.
> - **Scale/test**: All have free trials—test with a real client project. Prioritize unlimited guests/clients (huge for agencies) and integrations if you track changes in another system.
> - **General better practices**: Set clear feedback deadlines in the tool, use tags/statuses, combine with Loom for "why" explanations, and have a simple approval sign-off step. This reduces vague "make changes" requests dramatically vs. email/Slack.
> 
> Pastel is a great recommendation from your friend for getting started—many love it—but for handling both websites **and** designs at any scale without frustration, the options above (especially Webvizio or BugHerd) are the upgrades most teams make.
